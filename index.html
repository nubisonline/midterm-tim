Differences with open teams

While there aren't a lot of studies dealing with closed virtual teams (their occurence is relatively new), there's more studies about open teams. We would like to be able to use those results, so we're going to look at the differences, and hopefully similarity between open and closed teams. So, to answer the question Do closed teams experience the same frustrations w.r.t. communication (tools) as open teams?, we looked at two existing studies.

Slacking off

(Bin Lin, Alexey Zagalsky, Margaret-Anne Storey, and Alexander Serebrenik. 2016. Why Developers Are Slacking Off: Understanding How Software Teams Use Slack.)

Thanks, Bin
  • How often do you use slack?
  • What do you use slack for?
  • What is it good for?
  • What are your frustrations?

Hanging out

28 responses, 86% developer responses. Everyone is using Hangouts, always. People like it because it's faster than email, mostly positive about IM itself. Slack seems more of a social hub, news/info aggr. Customer support. Too noisy, chats while not working, client issues (images/videos). Fun.

Participatory Culture

(Margaret-Anne Storey, Leif Singer, Fernando Figueira Filho, Alexey Zagalsky, and Daniel M. German. 2016. How Social and Communication Channels Shape and Challenge a Participatory Culture in Software Development)

Alexey, thanks. One of contributions is challenges. Miscommunication text-based, people in general are a challenge. Self-control/unplugging related to survey.

So...

Sub-summary. Over the whole, it seems like the challenges facing these two types of teams are quite similar. Different use case. Slack survey use as personal stuff, news, etc. While full business focus (though some people use it for personal).
